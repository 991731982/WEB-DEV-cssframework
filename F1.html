ABOUT FORMULA 1

Formula 1, also known as F1, is the highest class of single-seater auto racing sanctioned by the Fédération Internationale de l'Automobile (FIA). The F1 season consists of a series of races, known as Grands Prix, held worldwide on both purpose-built circuits and public roads. It represents the pinnacle of driving skill, engineering excellence, and strategic prowess.

HISTORY OF FORMULA 1

1950s - The Beginning

The first world championship race was held at Silverstone in 1950, marking the official start of Formula 1 as a racing series.

->

1960s - Technological Advancements

With the introduction of aerodynamics and turbocharged engines, the 1960s marked a period of significant technological progress in F1.

1980s - The Turbo Era

The 1980s were dominated by turbocharged engines, making the cars faster and more powerful than ever before.

2000s - Modern Era

The modern era of F1 has seen remarkable technological innovation, with the introduction of hybrid engines and advanced aerodynamics, shaping the future of motor racing.
